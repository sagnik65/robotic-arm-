The Project Build & Documentation

About the Project

This project documents the entire process of building a 4-axis robotic arm from scratch. It covers the mechanical build, the electronics, and the creation of a multi-page web dashboard to monitor and control the arm's every move. All files, from the Arduino sketch to this website's code, are open-source.

Bill of Materials (BOM)

Component Quantity Notes
Acrylic 4-axis arm kit 1 A great starting point for the frame.
MG996R Servos 4 Good torque for the price. Upgrade shoulder for more power.
Arduino Uno R3 1 The brain of the operation.
5V 3A DC Power Supply 1 Essential for powering servos separately.

The Build Log

Part 1: Unboxing & Assembly

The journey begins with the arrival of the acrylic kit. This section details the initial unboxing, parts check, and the step-by-step mechanical assembly of the arm's frame, from the base to the gripper.

Part 2: Electronics & Wiring

Here, we wire the brain. This part covers connecting the four servo motors to the Arduino and the external power supply. Special attention is given to creating a common ground and managing the power distribution cleanly.
